Socket v1.01

new

Imagine you're a receptionist at a busy hotel. Your job is to connect guests in different rooms who want to communicate with each other. You have a special switchboard with many jacks, each representing a room. When a guest wants to talk to another guest, they pick up the phone in their room and tell you the room number they want to connect with. Just as you connect guests, sockets in computer networks act like these jacks on your switchboard. They're like virtual plugs and jacks that allow different programs (or applications) running on different computers to talk to each other. Sockets ensure that data from one program can be sent to and received by another program, even if they're on different computers in the same hotel (network). In this way, sockets serve as the behind-the-scenes communication system, enabling seamless data exchange between computers, much like your switchboard connects hotel guests' calls. They play a fundamental role in making sure information flows smoothly in the world of computer networks
